Spinosaurus

The Spinosaurus in Ark: Survival Evolved is a large, semi-aquatic predator that roams rivers and lakes. Highly aggressive and dangerous, it rivals the Rex in raw power but stands out for its speed and adaptability both on land and in water. When submerged, the Spino gains a boost to movement and attack strength, making it especially lethal in aquatic environments.
Taming a Spino requires tranquilizers and a steady supply of prime meat or kibble. Though challenging, the reward is a highly versatile mount. The Spino can switch between quadrupedal stance for faster mobility and bipedal stance for stronger attacks, allowing survivors to adapt to different combat situations with ease.
Because of its unique balance of power, speed, and adaptability, the Spinosaurus is a favorite among survivors for exploration, combat, and survival across diverse terrains.

Rex

The Tyrannosaurus Rex in Ark: Survival Evolved is one of the most iconic and fearsome predators on the island. Found roaming plains, forests, and mountains, the Rex is a highly aggressive carnivore that dominates its surroundings with raw power and massive size. Its roar and presence make it a constant threat to both survivors and wild creatures.
Taming a Rex requires tranquilizers and a steady supply of prime meat or kibble. While the process can be dangerous due to its strength and aggression, a successfully tamed Rex becomes a powerful ally. Its incredible damage output, high health, and intimidating roar make it a staple for large-scale battles and boss fights.
The Rex is prized by survivors as one of the best war mounts in the game. With its unmatched combat abilities and durability, it is commonly used in raids, boss encounters, and for defending bases. Its sheer dominance ensures that it remains a top choice for players seeking strength and reliability.

Raptor

The Raptor in Ark: Survival Evolved is a small but highly agile and intelligent predator. Found in packs across various biomes, Raptors are known for their speed, cunning, and pack hunting tactics. They are less powerful than larger predators but make up for it with their ability to quickly close distances and overwhelm prey through coordinated attacks.
Taming a Raptor requires tranquilizers and a supply of meat or kibble. While they are easier to tame compared to larger dinosaurs, their pack behavior means that taming one may attract others, leading to potentially dangerous situations. Once tamed, Raptors become fast and nimble mounts, ideal for scouting and quick strikes.
Raptors are favored by survivors for their speed and agility. They excel in hit-and-run tactics and can navigate difficult terrain with ease. Their pack mentality also allows them to be effective in group combat scenarios, making them valuable companions for both exploration and battle.

Dodo

The Dodo in Ark: Survival Evolved is a harmless, flightless bird that roams beaches and grassy areas. Unlike most creatures on the island, the Dodo poses no threat to survivors and is often one of the first animals encountered by new players. Its small size and slow movement make it an easy target for hunting or taming.
Taming a Dodo is very simple, requiring only a few berries or other basic foods. Because of this, many survivors tame them early in the game for practice or as a light source of eggs and meat. They can also be bred quickly, providing a reliable source of Dodo Eggs for crafting kibble.
While not useful in combat, the Dodo has a charm of its own. Survivors often keep them as pets, egg producers, or even as part of amusing base defenses by breeding large flocks. Despite its lack of strength, the Dodo has earned a place as one of the most recognizable and beloved creatures in the game.

Ankylosaurus

The Ankylosaurus in Ark: Survival Evolved is a heavily armored herbivore known for its distinctive clubbed tail and robust body. Found in various biomes, including forests and plains, the Ankylosaurus is a peaceful creature that primarily feeds on plants. Its thick armor provides excellent protection against predators, making it a reliable companion for survivors.
Taming an Ankylosaurus requires tranquilizers and a supply of vegetables or kibble. Once tamed, it becomes an invaluable asset for resource gathering, particularly for harvesting metal, stone, and other hard materials due to its powerful tail swipe attack. This makes the Ankylosaurus a favorite among survivors looking to efficiently collect resources while minimizing risk.
In addition to its resource-gathering capabilities, the Ankylosaurus is also a sturdy mount that can withstand significant damage in combat situations. Its defensive nature and ability to protect itself make it a dependable choice for both new and experienced players alike.
